About FPV Drones

First Person View (FPV) flying is an immersive way to pilot drones by viewing real-time video transmitted from a camera mounted on the aircraft. This technology has revolutionized drone racing, freestyle flying, and aerial cinematography.

Key Characteristics of FPV Systems

  • Low Latency: Minimal delay between camera and display for responsive control
  • High Performance: Components designed for agility and speed
  • Modular Design: Easy to upgrade and customize individual components
  • Reliability: Built to withstand crashes and harsh flying conditions

Flight Controllers

What is a Flight Controller?

The flight controller (FC) is the central processing unit of your drone. It's a sophisticated computer that reads data from various sensors (gyroscope, accelerometer, barometer) and calculates the appropriate motor speeds to maintain stability and execute pilot commands.

Key Features

  • Processing Power: Modern FCs use F4, F7, or H7 processors
  • IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit): High-precision gyroscope and accelerometer
  • Firmware: Betaflight, KISS, or other flight control software
  • Connectivity: UART ports for peripherals, USB for configuration
  • Voltage Regulators: Built-in BEC (Battery Eliminator Circuit) for powering accessories

Choosing a Flight Controller

Consider these factors:

  • Frame size and mounting pattern (20x20mm, 30.5x30.5mm, etc.)
  • Processor speed (F4 for basic, F7/H7 for advanced features)
  • Number of UART ports needed for peripherals
  • Integrated OSD capability
  • Voltage requirements and BEC specifications

Electronic Speed Controllers (ESCs)

What is an ESC?

Electronic Speed Controllers (ESCs) are devices that regulate the speed and direction of brushless motors. They convert DC battery power into three-phase AC power that drives the motors, responding to signals from the flight controller.

Key Features

  • Current Rating: Measured in amps (e.g., 30A, 45A, 60A per ESC)
  • Firmware: BLHeli_S, BLHeli_32, or manufacturer-specific
  • DSHOT Protocol: Modern digital communication (DSHOT300, DSHOT600)
  • 4-in-1 or Individual: Integrated stack or separate ESCs
  • BEC: Some include voltage regulators for FC power

Choosing an ESC

Consider these factors:

  • Current rating should exceed motor draw by 20-30%
  • Voltage rating compatible with battery (2-6S)
  • Firmware compatibility (BLHeli_32 recommended)
  • DSHOT protocol support for better performance
  • Mounting hole pattern matching your frame

Brushless Motors

What are Brushless Motors?

Brushless motors are the workhorses of FPV drones, converting electrical energy into mechanical rotation. They're called "brushless" because they use electronic commutation instead of mechanical brushes, offering higher efficiency, reliability, and power.

Understanding Motor Specifications

  • Size: Expressed as XXYY (e.g., 2207) - XX is stator width, YY is stator height in mm
  • KV Rating: RPM per volt (higher KV = higher speed, lower torque)
  • Stator Volume: Larger volume = more torque and power
  • Configuration: Number of stator poles and magnets (e.g., 12N14P)
  • Shaft Size: Typically M5 for props 5" and above

Motor Selection Tips

Consider these factors:

  • Match motor size to frame and propeller size
  • Higher KV for lighter props, lower KV for heavier props
  • Check motor mounting pattern (16x16mm or 19x19mm)
  • Consider weight - heavier motors affect flight characteristics
  • Quality matters - good bearings and magnets ensure reliability

Drone Frames

What is a Drone Frame?

The frame is the structural backbone of your FPV drone. It provides mounting points for all components and must be strong enough to survive crashes while remaining light enough for optimal flight performance. Modern frames are typically made from carbon fiber.

Frame Specifications

  • Size Class: Measured by diagonal motor-to-motor distance or prop size
  • Material: Carbon fiber thickness (3mm, 4mm, 5mm for arms)
  • Geometry: X, H, Stretch-X, DeadCat configurations
  • Mounting Pattern: 20x20mm, 30.5x30.5mm stack mounting
  • Camera Angle: Adjustable mount for different flying styles

Choosing a Frame

Consider these factors:

  • Flying style - racing frames are light, freestyle frames are durable
  • Arm thickness - 4mm for racing, 5-6mm for freestyle
  • Camera protection - top plate coverage
  • Electronics stack space - room for FC, ESC, VTX
  • Propeller protection - optional ducted frames for safety

FPV Cameras

What is an FPV Camera?

FPV cameras are specialized analog or digital cameras designed for low-latency video transmission. Unlike action cameras (GoPro), FPV cameras prioritize minimal delay over high resolution, essential for real-time piloting.

Key Features

  • Sensor: CCD or CMOS (CMOS is modern standard)
  • Resolution: Typically 600-1200 TVL (TV Lines)
  • Latency: Ultra-low, usually under 10ms
  • Dynamic Range: WDR (Wide Dynamic Range) for mixed lighting
  • Size: Micro (14mm), Mini (19mm), Standard (28mm)
  • Voltage: 5V input typically

Camera Selection

Consider these factors:

  • Sensor size affects low-light performance
  • WDR helps in variable lighting conditions
  • Lens FOV (field of view) - wider for racing, narrower for cruising
  • PAL vs NTSC standard (PAL = 50Hz, NTSC = 60Hz)
  • Physical size must fit frame's camera mount

Video Transmitters (VTX)

What is a VTX?

A Video Transmitter (VTX) broadcasts the video signal from your FPV camera to your goggles or ground station. It operates on specific frequency bands regulated by local laws, typically in the 5.8GHz range for FPV use.

Key Features

  • Frequency: 5.8GHz (most common), 2.4GHz, 1.3GHz available
  • Power Output: 25mW to 1000mW+ (check local regulations)
  • Channels: Multiple bands (A, B, E, F, R) with 8 channels each
  • SmartAudio/Tramp: Change settings via OSD
  • Pit Mode: Low power mode for bench testing

VTX Selection

Consider these factors:

  • Power output - higher isn't always better (legal limits, interference)
  • Frequency bands - ensure compatibility with goggles
  • SmartAudio/Tramp support for easy configuration
  • Mounting size and weight
  • Antenna connector type (MMCX, U.FL, SMA)
